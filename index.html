Due diligence requirements
MF — Low
Daily NAV, SEBI-regulated, fully transparent
Track fund vs benchmark. Monitor fund manager changes. Check expense ratio (regular vs direct). No lock-in for most funds. Client suitability = simple KYC + risk profile.
PMS — Medium
Strategy monitoring, concentration risk, manager track record
Review monthly factsheets + quarterly reports. Verify portfolio overlap. Watch for style drift. Understand performance fee triggers. Min ticket ₹50L (HNI-only clients). Separate client account — reduces counterparty risk.
AIF — High
Illiquid structures, complex legal docs, drawdown schedules
Read PPM carefully. Cat I/II: closed-ended, capital call risk, 3–7 yr lock-in. Cat III: leverage, complex derivatives, mark-to-model valuation. Verify fund manager SEBI registration. Min ticket ₹1 Cr. Know exit/liquidity windows. Higher fiduciary duty on distributor.
Key caveats
MF trail is recurring and passive
Once invested, trail accrues monthly regardless of activity. SEBI's TER caps limit upside but also protect consistency.
PMS income = fixed + variable
Fixed referral is reliable. Performance fee share is negotiated deal-by-deal and may not exist at all managers.
AIF: illiquidity means lumpy income
Capital is called in tranches. Income accrues on deployed capital, not total commitment. Cat III income is more linear.
Regulatory note
Distributors must comply with AMFI (MF) and SEBI (PMS/AIF) registration. Trail fees must be disclosed to clients under SEBI norms.
